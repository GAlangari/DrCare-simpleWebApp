Hypertension

Hypertension is a disease When there is constant pressure on the artery walls for a long time, it usually has no symptoms, but it can cause serious problems such as: stroke, heart and kidney failure. It is caused by an increased workload on the heart and blood vessels. Detection is based on reading blood pressure measurements. It can be controlled by following a healthy lifestyle and taking medications (if needed), so, its treatment - based on the diagnosis - fouces on on having a healthy lifestyle in general. [1]

Diabetes

It is a chronic disease that affects the way the body's cells receive glucose or the amount of insulin secreted by the pancreas, leading to an abnormal high or low in the level of blood sugar. It has two types: First one is Type 1 diabetes which is the lack of secretion of insulin or its absence at all due to damage to the beta cells in the pancreas, which makes the patient need to obtain insulin from an external source for life. Second one is Type 2 diabetes which the body's resistance to the effects of insulin, or that the beta cells do not make enough of it. [1]

Obesity

Obesity can be defined as an excessive or abnormal increase of fat that damages an individual's health. The main cause of weight gain and obesity: the energy imbalance between the calories that enter the body and the calories it burns. The main causes of obesity can be: family history of illness, the nature of an individual or family's dietary pattern, Absence or lack of doing exercise, Some medications, such as: antidepressants, unorganized sleep system, and other causes related to the lifestyle of the individual. The treatment include: individual's dietary changes, doing exercises regularly, and Change the wrong behaviors in general. [1]
